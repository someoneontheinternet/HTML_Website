Introduction

The Arctic Region is among the uttermost interesting places on Earth. The region is composed of three territories: Yukon, NorthWest Territory, Nunavut confederated on July 1870. The region contains a large amount of diversity in terrains, climates, culture, and wildlife. With the current population of around 75,249, the arctic region is only 8% of the population in Vancouver. However, with the region mainly focusing on mining as one of the most important aspects of their economy, the region is still productive. The region also has a contrasting climate since it is situated inland with freezing Atlantic air blowing across the region. Most of the time, the temperature is pretty extreme reaching -40C in the winter, while 25C in the Summer. During the summer time, the territory can attract ten’s of thousands of Tourist all around the world to view the beautiful scenery. Agriculture is not a huge part of the economy since permafrost covers a huge area of land rendering the upper north part extremely difficult to farm. Overall, the Arctics is still one of the most interesting places.

Future Prospects

The past few years the arctic has been a very important area in the talks of the future of natural resources, mostly oil because of its high value in the market. Geothermal energy has also been an option that the world has been considering. The field is open even more north of us but we still have a big part of it under the land of Canada. Furthermore, because of global warming, the thick layers of ice have been thawing meaning the option of using the ocean as a giant highway is becoming more and more possible. Now that we know the land is filled with oil, and that we have a way of transporting it to almost anywhere by sea. My estimate that follows most professional opinions is that Canada will go forth in the mining operations in the northern part of their country. By doing this we are opening more jobs in a less populated area giving growth to our economy. And this is only the tip of the iceberg, most of the area is untouched there is no real number of how much oil is accessible for us to take. And if we are going to compare our arctic to the Russian arctic we can expect more than just oil, other minerals like nickel, gold, silver, zinc platinum, cobalt, and more rare stuff that is revealing itself now that the permafrost is melting, things like fossils are appearing more and more. The only problem that we are currently facing is the amount of money that it cost to expand into a new area, first off you must set down your foundations and you must pay people to stay at camps. Won't be cheap because who wants to live in the middle of nowhere? But the Future in the Canadian Arctic will be crazy because of all the riches we have yet to unbury.

Interesting Facts and Places

Interesting Facts

  1. The word “Arctic” came from the Greek word “arktikos” Which means bear. That is because the constellation “Ursa Major- the great bear” can be seen in the Northern sky.
  2. At least one day in the arctic is 24 hours day (summer) and 24 hours night (winter).
  3. If all the ice in the arctic melted, the world’s sea level will rise by 24 feet.
  4. The Arctic Ocean covers about 5.4 million square miles, which is bigger than the whole Europe.
